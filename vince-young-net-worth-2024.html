Vince Young Net Worth 2024: How the Former NFL Star Lost His Fortune

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Monday, July 15, 2024
Vince Young is a former NFL quarterback who played for six seasons in the league, earning over $34 million in salary and $30 million in endorsements.

He was a college football legend who led the University of Texas to a national championship in 2006 and was drafted as the No. 3 pick by the Tennessee Titans later that year.

He won several awards, including the NFL Rookie of the Year and the AP NFL Offensive Rookie of the Year in 2006, and was a two-time Pro Bowl selection. However, his career declined after a series of injuries, conflicts, and poor performances, and he was out of the league by 2014.

Early Life

Vincent Paul Young Jr. was born on May 18, 1982, in Houston, Texas. Felicia, his mother, and his grandmother reared him. His father, Vincent Sr., was sentenced to prison in 2003 after being convicted of burglary.

When a car struck Vince while he was riding his bicycle, he was 7 years old. Young was hospitalized for months after the accident because the handlebar went into his stomach. He attended Dick Dowling Middle School and Madison High School, where he was a three-year quarterback on the football team.

How Vince Young Wasted His Money on Lavish Spending

One of the main reasons why Vince Young lost his fortune was his extravagant spending habits. According to various reports, Young spent $5,000 a week at The Cheesecake Factory, a restaurant chain that he loved.

He also once bought all the seats on a Southwest Airlines flight because he wanted privacy. He spent millions on cars, jewelry, parties, and gifts for his friends and family.

He even paid for the entire bill of his teammates at a steakhouse, which amounted to $6,000. Young did not keep track of his expenses or budget his money, and he trusted his financial advisor and his uncle, whom he hired as his manager, to handle his finances.

What is Vince Young Net Worth in 2024?

Vince Young’s net worth in 2024 is estimated to be $400,000. This is a fraction of what he earned during his NFL career and a far cry from his peak net worth of $25 million in 2007.

Young has been inducted into the Texas Sports Hall of Fame, the Rose Bowl Hall of Fame, and the College Football Hall of Fame, and he has a charitable foundation that supports underprivileged children. However, his financial troubles have tarnished his legacy and serve as a cautionary tale for other athletes who make millions at a young age.

Vince Young Personal Life

Vince married his high school girlfriend, Candice Johnson, on April 15, 2012, and they have a son named Jordan. On January 10, 2006, Houston Mayor Bill White declared January 10th “Vince Young Day,” and on February 20, 2007, the Texas Senate passed a resolution making “Vince Young Day” a statewide observance. Young re-enrolled at the University of Texas in 2008, and he graduated in 2013 with a degree in youth and community studies.
