How did Tom Berenger start his Professional Career?

Before working as a flight attendant for Eastern Airlines in San Juan, Puerto Rico, in 1972, Tom performed in regional theater. In 1973, he moved to New York. This personality chose “Berenger” as his professional name after being obliged to change his surname because there was already a “Tom Moore” in the Actors’ Equity Association.

He made appearances in soap operas and played Tim Siegel, a lawyer, on One Life to Live. In a similar vein, Rush It was his first feature film (1976). In the 1979 movie Butch and Sundance: The Early Days, Tom also played Butch Cassidy. Tom’s portrayal of Staff Sergeant Bob Barnes in Platoon (1986) earned him a nomination for an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor. He is also well-known for playing Thomas Beckett in Sniper and Jake Taylor in Major League.
