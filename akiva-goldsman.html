Akiva Goldsman is an American screenwriter, director, and producer of motion pictures and adaptations of popular novels. Goldsman’s filmography as a screenwriter includes The Client; Batman Forever and its sequel Batman & Robin; I, Robot; I Am Legend; Cinderella Man, and numerous rewrites that are both credited and uncredited. He also wrote more than a dozen episodes for the science fiction television series Fringe.

In 2002, Goldsman won the Academy Award for Best Adapted Screenplay and the Golden Globe Award for Best Screenplay for A Beautiful Mind, which took home both the Academy Award for Best Picture and the Golden Globe. In 2006, after reteaming with Ron Howard to adapt Dan Brown’s book The Da Vinci Code, he wrote the film adaptation of the same name. He also penned Angels & Demons (2009), its sequel.

He is also the creator of Star Trek: Strange New Worlds, a prequel to Star Trek: The Original Series that will premiere on CBS All Access in May 2020. In addition, he co-created the DC Comics TV series Titans and the Paramount Plus series Star Trek: Picard, a sequel to Star Trek: The Next Generation and Star Trek: Nemesis. He’s also worked on the upcoming offering Star Trek: Strange New Worlds, which is set before the events of Star Trek: The Original Series. Akiva Goldsman Career

Goldsman’s career began in the late 1980s when he was asked to write a treatment for the film Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles. From there, he went on to pen the screenplay for the 1992 film Kuffs and co-write the screenplay for the 1993 hit Jurassic Park III. In 1995, he made his directorial debut with the action thriller The client. Goldsman’s other notable writing credits include Batman Forever, Batman & Robin, I, Robot, I Am Legend, and Cinderella Man. He has also produced such films as The Da Vinci Code, Angels & Demons, and Star Trek: Picard.
